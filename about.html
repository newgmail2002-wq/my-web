How I work

Four rules I don't break

I tell you when I'm the wrong person

If your project needs something I'm not the best choice for, you'll hear it in the first conversation — not three weeks and one invoice later. I'd rather lose the job than do it badly. It costs me work and it's still the right call.

No jargon, ever

If I can't explain what I'm building and why it's worth the money in plain English, I don't understand it well enough to charge you for it.

You own everything

Your code, your accounts, your data, your domain — all in your name. No lock-in, no hostage situation, no "we host it for you" trap.

Judged on outcomes

Not on how pretty the mockup was. Leads booked, calls answered, ads holding their cost per result. If it doesn't move a number, it isn't finished.
