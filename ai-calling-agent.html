The honest version

You're not losing leads. You're losing the ones who called while you were busy.

Count the calls you missed last week — the ones that rang out while you were with a customer, driving, asleep, or it was Sunday. Most of those people didn't leave a voicemail and they didn't call back. They just called the next business on the list.

An AI calling agent picks up every one of them. It sounds human, it holds a real conversation, it knows your prices and your hours, it qualifies whether the person is worth your time, and it books them straight into your calendar. Then it sends you the transcript. It costs less than a part-time receptionist and it works 168 hours a week.

What you get

Included as standard

  • A voice that doesn't sound like a robot
    Natural speech, natural pauses, handles interruptions. Most callers won't clock it — and it will tell them it's an assistant if they ask.
  • Every call answered, on the first ring, always
    No hold music, no voicemail, no 'we're closed'. Nights, weekends, holidays, and while you're already on another call.
  • Leads qualified before they reach you
    It asks the questions you'd ask — budget, timeline, location, what they actually need — so you only spend your time on the people worth it.
  • Booked straight into your calendar
    It checks your real availability and books the slot during the call. No back-and-forth, no double bookings, no lead going cold while you play phone tag.
  • A transcript and summary of every conversation
    Read what was said, spot what people keep asking about, and catch anything the agent should handle better. Full visibility, not a black box.
  • Wired into the tools you already use
    Your calendar, your CRM, your inbox, WhatsApp. It drops the lead where your team already works instead of creating another dashboard to check.
  • Outbound too, if you want it
    Follow up with old enquiries, confirm appointments, chase no-shows — the calls nobody on your team ever gets around to making.
  • A human handover when it matters
    It knows what it doesn't know. Anything complex or sensitive gets routed to a real person with the context attached.

Questions

AI Calling Agent FAQ

Will callers know it's an AI?
Many won't notice, and it's built to be upfront rather than deceptive — if someone asks whether they're talking to a person, it says it's an assistant. Some regions also require disclosure on recorded or AI-handled calls, and I'll set that up correctly for where you operate.
What if it can't answer something?
It hands off to a human with the full context, or takes a message and flags it — whichever you prefer. It's built to escalate rather than guess, because a confident wrong answer is worse than 'let me get someone for you'.
Can it use my phone number?
Yes. It can take over your existing number, sit on a new one, or only pick up calls you don't answer within a few rings — so your team still gets first shot.
What does it cost to run?
There's the build, and then usage-based costs for the voice and telephony — you pay for the minutes it actually handles. I'll give you a realistic monthly estimate based on your call volume before you commit, not after.
How long does it take to set up?
Typically one to two weeks, depending on how many systems it connects to and how much of your business logic it needs to learn. You'll test it thoroughly before it ever answers a real customer.
What languages does it speak?
Plenty, including English, Hindi and Punjabi — and it can switch mid-call if your caller does.
