Questions

Before you ask

What does an AI calling agent actually do?
It answers your phone in a natural human voice, 24/7. It qualifies leads, books appointments straight into your calendar, answers common questions, and follows up with people who never called back — then hands you a transcript of every conversation. It doesn't get tired, it doesn't miss a call at 2am, and it costs a fraction of a receptionist.
What's the difference between UGC ads and AI ads?
UGC ads are creator-style videos that look like a real person recommending your product — they build trust precisely because they don't look like ads. AI ads are generated visuals and video built from your product and brand, which lets you test twenty concepts in the time it used to take to shoot one. Most brands win by running both: UGC for trust, AI for volume and speed.
Do you work with clients outside India?
Yes. I work with brands worldwide and I'm used to running projects across time zones. Everything is handled remotely — calls, files, revisions and handover — and I work in English, Hindi and Punjabi.
How much does a website or AI project cost?
Every project is scoped and quoted individually, because a five-page brochure site and an AI agent wired into your CRM aren't the same job. Tell me what you're trying to achieve through the contact form and you'll get a clear, itemised quote — no obligation, no pressure.
How long does a project take?
A typical website runs two to four weeks from brief to launch. Ad creative usually turns around in a few days. An AI calling agent takes roughly one to two weeks depending on how many systems it needs to connect to. You get a firm timeline in writing before any work starts.
Will my website actually rank on Google?
Every site I build ships with clean semantic HTML, proper meta and Open Graph tags, structured data, a sitemap, fast load times and mobile-first layout — the technical foundation Google rewards. Ranking also depends on your content and your competition, so I'll tell you honestly what's realistic for your market rather than promise you page one.
