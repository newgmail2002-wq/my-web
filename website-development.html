The honest version

Speed isn't a feature. It's the whole foundation.

Every extra second your site takes to load costs you customers — and Google measures that second too. Most sites are slow for the same reason: they're a template carrying twelve plugins, four tracking scripts and a page builder that ships megabytes of code to render a heading.

I build the opposite. Only what your site needs, structured so browsers render it fast and crawlers understand it immediately. This site you're reading right now is the reference: no frameworks, no libraries, and it still does everything you can see.

What you get

Included as standard

  • Sub-second loads, honestly measured
    Optimised images, minimal payload, no dead weight. Tested on real connections and mid-range phones, not just a fast laptop on office wifi.
  • Technical SEO baked in
    Semantic HTML, structured data, clean meta and Open Graph tags, XML sitemap, canonical URLs. The foundation Google rewards — from line one, not bolted on later.
  • Genuinely responsive
    Every breakpoint checked on real devices. Not 'it works on my machine' — it works on the six-year-old Android your customer is holding.
  • Accessible by default
    Keyboard navigation, screen-reader labels, proper contrast, reduced-motion support. Better for people, and Google counts it too.
  • A CMS if you want one — and none if you don't
    Edit your own content without breaking the design. Or stay fully static for maximum speed and zero maintenance. Your call, and I'll tell you which one actually fits.
  • Deploys anywhere, owned by you
    Your code, your hosting, your accounts. No proprietary platform holding your site hostage.

Questions

Website Development FAQ

What do you build with?
Whatever genuinely fits the job — often clean HTML, CSS and JavaScript with no framework, because it's the fastest thing that can possibly work. Bigger builds get a framework or CMS. I'll explain the choice in plain English rather than hiding behind stack names.
Do you work with WordPress, Shopify or Webflow?
Yes, when they're the right tool — Shopify for serious ecommerce, WordPress when a team needs a familiar editor. But I won't reach for a heavy platform when a fast, simple site would serve you better.
Can you fix my slow existing website?
Often, yes. I'll audit it first and tell you honestly whether it's worth optimising or whether you'd get more from a rebuild — including when the honest answer is 'leave it alone, it's fine'.
What happens after launch?
You get a walkthrough, the source, and the accounts in your name. I'm around for support if you want it, but you're never locked in.
